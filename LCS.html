Life Changing Summer 2016

In July 2016, I took part in the three-week Life Changing Summer at Cornell, a program focused on entrepreneurship and web development. The main goal was to develop and present a viable business idea by the end of the course, when a pitch competition would be held. I had a very good time during my stay, but I also invested quite a bit of work into seeing my project through.

The project

My plan was to build an electronics learning kit and market it to high schools, in an attempt to introduce students to the world of circuit design in an enjoyable and engaging way. The idea ended up winning the final pitch competition.

I decided to structure the content akin to the levels in a puzzle game. An automatic evaluation would assess the real world circuit built by the player and provide feedback on whether it fulfilled the level's goal, or provide hints if the player was stuck. A real-time oscilloscope screen would also be included to aid trial and error approaches. For the demo protoype I presented a level in which the player was supposed to build a 555 Timer circuit to generate a PWM signal with the correct duty cycle. For the evaluation device, I used an Arduino that sampled the user signal with its on-board ADC.
