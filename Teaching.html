Teaching Underpriviliged Children Science and Tennis

Not so long ago, I found out that the school dropout rate is a worrying 17.5 percent in Romania, and this made me ask myself whether I could make a difference.

In 2016, I volunteered for two months as an informal science teacher in an attempt to help some of the children who face the risk of school abandonment. I taught physics and chemistry exclusively through experiments.

The experience was a success - I feel that I have motivated a considerable number of my students to become more engaged at school. Regardless of their level of formal science preparation, most seemed very impressed by what I presented and even asked meaningful questions. The supervisors who attended my lessons were equally captivated and should be able to reproduce the experiments in the future, as they only require household supplies.

As I am an avid tennis player, I also taught several sports lessons. It was a bit hard for me to get used to playing with beginners, but I managed to match their playing style in the end.
