Infineon Robot Building Workshops 2016

In the 2016 edition of the workshops, my team, though not entirely the original one, tried to fix all of the shortcomings from the previous year. We won the final contest.

Design

We started by implementing a two board design instead of one, so as to increase the distance between the sensors and the wheels. This gave the controller more reaction time and made tuning the PID easier. We also included some luxury, namely a battery indicator and more blinkenlights. We also finally figured out how to implement the integrative term (I in PID). Clamping its influence at a certain threshold eliminates the instability it would have caused otherwise. Unfortunately, heavier batteries were mandatory this time, which negated some of the improvements we introduced.
