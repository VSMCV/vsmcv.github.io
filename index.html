My name is Vlad Smarandache and I'm a sophomore studying Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell. This is the place to find out more about my projects, my interests, or just random things that seem to be worth sharing.

I care about Electrical Engineering

There are very many reasons why love my field of engineering. At first, when I started tinkering with circuits in high school, it seemed to strike the perfect balance between accesibility and grounding in the real, physical world. Even though anyone can get into computer science for nothing more than the price of a laptop, it was never enough for me to see the results of an algorithm show up on my screen, or the debugging messages if my program errored out. I want to witness a contraption move, a light bulb come on, or smell the magic smoke if I fail. And even though any other field would be just as easy to get into now, as a college student, my fascination with electrical engineering has never vanished. On the contrary, having discovered the whole breadth of ECE, from digital logic and signal processing to analog circuits, I have developed a better sense of its importance. I can now see ECE almost everywhere I look, with the same principles and ways of thinking used to create technology of all kinds.

Background

I studied at the International Computer High School of Bucharest, focusing at first on Chemistry. I became better in physics and computer science starting in 10th grade, after I realized that I wanted to continue with Electrical Engineering in college. Through my personal projects and involvement in various robotics activities, I have already gained quite a bit of practical experience to support what I'm learning in class. I am confident in my ability to both design PCBs and write software for embedded applications.
